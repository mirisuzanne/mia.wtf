All we ever wanted was _more agency_
in our digital lives --
something the web was intended to help with.
But what we keep getting is _digital agents_
that bury us in slop and bureaucracy.

<!-- intro -->

There's nothing inherently wrong with
_automation_ or even '_machine learning_'.
But I have no interest in language-guessing machines
built on surveillance and exploited labor
and abandoned climate goals --
sold back to us as a replacement for expertise.

I have no interest in trusting
a black-box information-laundering machine
built by weapons contractors
and the worst guys on Sir Tim's World Wide Web.

It's a product designed to reframe bias
as hidden truth,
and deflect accountability
onto a fancy slab of electrified rocks.
Some people will tell you that _it works_,
and I agree.
But we disagree about what it's designed to do.

**Everything on this site
was put here by me --
a human --
because it's something I want to share.
This website reflects my _point of view_.**
